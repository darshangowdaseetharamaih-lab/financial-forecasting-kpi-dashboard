## Section 3: Customer Segment Distribution

### What You're Seeing

A pie chart showing customer count and revenue by segment:

| Segment | Customers | Revenue | % of Total |
|---------|-----------|---------|------------|
| Enterprise | 45 | $1.1M | 45% |
| SMB | 320 | $850K | 35% |
| Startup | 580 | $350K | 14% |
| Individual | 1,250 | $150K | 6% |

### What Changed

Enterprise segment grew from 38% to 45% of revenue share, driven by:
- 3 new enterprise accounts closed
- Expansion within existing enterprise customers
- Enterprise deal sizes increased 15%

### Why It Matters

**Revenue concentration** is increasing in enterprise segment:
- **Opportunity**: Higher LTV, stickier relationships, expansion potential
- **Risk**: Dependence on fewer customers, longer sales cycles

The **Pareto principle** is evident: 2% of customers (Enterprise) generate 45% of revenue.

### Recommended Actions

1. **Protect enterprise base**: Assign dedicated CSMs, monitor health scores
2. **Build pipeline**: Diversify enterprise accounts to reduce concentration risk
3. **SMB efficiency**: Automate SMB segment for profitable scale
4. **Startup conversion**: Create clear upgrade path from Startup to SMB

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **LTV** | Customer Lifetime Value - total revenue from a customer over their relationship |
| **CAC** | Customer Acquisition Cost - cost to acquire one new customer |
| **Churn** | Rate at which customers stop using the product |
| **MoM** | Month-over-Month change |
| **Operating Leverage** | Revenue growth rate / Expense growth rate |
| **Gross Margin** | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue |
| **Net Margin** | Net Income / Revenue |
